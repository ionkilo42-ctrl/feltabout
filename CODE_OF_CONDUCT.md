# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in the Feltabout project a respectful, focused, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- giving constructive, technically grounded feedback
- respecting the project’s safety-first and non-clinical boundaries
- being direct without being hostile
- assuming good faith while still challenging unsafe or weak ideas
- accepting responsibility and correcting mistakes

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks
- sexualized language or unwanted sexual attention
- publishing private information without permission
- abusive, manipulative, or threatening conduct
- trying to steer the project into therapy claims, coercive use cases, or unsafe interpersonal tooling

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this code of conduct. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this code of conduct.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community.

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it through the repository’s maintainer contact path or open an issue requesting moderator attention. Do not post sensitive personal information publicly.
