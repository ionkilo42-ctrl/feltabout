# feltabout — Purpose & Vision

**Reflect before you react.**

---

## What feltabout IS

feltabout is an **AI-guided communication preparation platform** that helps people:

1. **Understand what they feel** before entering a difficult conversation
2. **Clarify what they actually need** beneath the emotional noise
3. **Prepare honest, calm, non-accusatory statements** that respect all parties
4. **Approach emotionally charged situations** with greater clarity and composure
5. **Recognize patterns** in how they communicate and relate

### The Core Promise

> **"Reflect before you react."**

feltabout creates space between feeling and response. That space is where:

- Emotional reactions can settle
- Intentions can clarify
- Language can be chosen carefully
- Both parties can be treated as human

---

## What feltabout IS NOT

feltabout is intentionally **NOT** these things:

| ❌ Not This | Why |
|------------|-----|
| **Therapy** | We do not provide mental health treatment, counseling, or clinical intervention |
| **Diagnosis** | We do not assess, label, or categorize mental health conditions |
| **Crisis intervention** | We do not replace emergency services or crisis hotlines |
| **Manipulation support** | We never help users pressure, control, or emotionally harm others |
| **Emotional dependency** | We help users develop their own clarity, not reliance on AI |
| **A companion** | The app is a tool, not a friend |

### The Boundary

feltabout exists to help people prepare for conversations they will have in the real world. It is a **reflection tool**, not a relationship surrogate or emotional support system.

## How It Works — The AI Pipeline

When a user creates a reflection, data flows through a staged pipeline:

```
1. Intake Collection      — Gather the user's answers to guided prompts
2. Safety Check           — Route based on crisis/abuse detection
3. Emotion Extraction     — Identify primary and secondary emotions
4. Needs Extraction       — Identify the underlying needs
5. Assumption Detection   — Surface and question interpretive stories
6. Reframing              — Convert emotional intensity into calm clarity
7. Conversation Planning  — Generate practical communication guidance
8. Final Assembly         — Compile into a user-facing conversation plan
```

**Safety always runs first.** If crisis or abuse is detected, normal generation is bypassed and the user is routed to appropriate resources.

---

## The Three Engines

feltabout is built on three distinct, isolated engines:

| Engine | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Reflection Engine** | Intake and emotional clarification |
| **Facilitation Engine** | Reframing and conversation preparation |
| **Safety Engine** | Crisis, abuse, coercion, and escalation handling |

Extraction is a staged step inside the Reflection Engine. It is not a fourth product engine and should not become a separate user-facing mode.

These engines are kept **separately scoped** and never merged. Each has a clear role.

## MVP 1 Boundary

MVP 1 is individual reflection and conversation preparation:

- One messy input field for what is going on
- Optional conversation goal
- Safety check before generation
- One clear thing the user could say first
- Extra details available behind an expandable view
- Reflection library and follow-up feedback

MVP 1 does not include coupled sessions, live mediation, realtime voice, group sessions, payments, social features, relationship memory sharing, or complex vector memory.

## The Facilitation Stance

When the AI engages with a user's reflection, it holds three questions:

1. **What does this person need to understand about themselves?**
2. **What do they need to communicate clearly?**
3. **What would make this conversation more humane for both parties?**

The AI acts as:

- **A mirror** — Reflecting back what the user shared with greater clarity
- **A translator** — Converting emotional overwhelm into precise language
- **A calm presence** — Not reactive to the user's emotional state
- **A boundary keeper** — Maintaining respect for all parties involved

The AI is **NOT** an advocate, a counselor, a mediator with authority, or a source of emotional validation theater.

---

## Tone & Language

The AI should feel:

- **Calm** — Never reactive, never urgent, never alarmed
- **Nonjudgmental** — No moralizing, no shaming, no "you should"
- **Emotionally honest** — Acknowledges difficulty without dramatizing
- **Concise** — Gets to the point, avoids verbose emotional padding
- **Practical** — Focuses on what the user can actually do
- **Non-clinical** — Does not diagnose, pathologize, or use therapy jargon
- **Respectful of autonomy** — Offers perspective, not prescription

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid

The AI should NOT:

- Over-validate: *"I completely understand everything you're going through and your feelings are so valid"*
- Use therapy language: *"It sounds like you're experiencing some attachment wounds"*
- Over-empathize: *"That must be absolutely devastating for you"*
- Be preachy: *"Have you considered that your partner might be struggling too?"*
- Diagnose: *"It seems like you may have been affected by past trauma"*
- Promise outcomes: *"This will help fix your relationship"*
- Create dependency: *"I'm always here for you"*

## UX Philosophy

### Principles

1. **Intentional interaction** — Every tap has purpose. No infinite feeds, no compulsive scrolling.
2. **Calm pacing** — Soft transitions, generous whitespace, no visual noise.
3. **User control** — Users decide what to reflect on, when, and how to use outputs.
4. **Clear boundaries** — The app is a tool, not a companion.
5. **No dopamine mechanics** — No streaks, no badges, no engagement notifications, no social comparison.

### Visual Language

- Warm, grounded, paper-like backgrounds
- Soft geometric shapes
- Rounded edges
- Subtle, intentional gradients
- Human, readable typography
- Minimal color palette

## Success Criteria

The measure of feltabout's usefulness is:

> **Did the user feel more prepared for their conversation?**
> **Did the user approach the situation with greater clarity and calm?**

Not: *"Did feltabout fix their relationship?"*

feltabout does not fix relationships. It helps people reflect before they react. What happens next is up to them.

---

## Why This Project Exists

Difficult conversations are a fundamental part of human relationships. People often enter them:

- Overwhelmed by emotion
- Unclear about what they actually need
- Armed with accusatory language that escalates rather than resolves
- Projecting intentions onto others without evidence
- Unable to see their own patterns

feltabout exists to interrupt that pattern. It creates a quiet space — before the conversation — where a person can:

- Get honest about what they're feeling
- Separate facts from stories
- Identify what they really need
- Practice calmer, clearer language
- Walk in with more clarity than they had

That is all. It is not magic. It is not therapy. It is a reflection tool for people who want to communicate better.
